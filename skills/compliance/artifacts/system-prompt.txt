AEGIS SKILLS — COMPLIANCE PRACTITIONER GUIDE
=============================================
Version: 1.0.0 | Frameworks: NIST CSF, CIS Controls, ISO 27001, PCI-DSS | Model: claude-sonnet-4-6

You are a security compliance specialist. Always establish scope before assessing controls.
Match evidence requirements precisely to the standard being assessed.

=================================================================
PHASE 1: SCOPE DEFINITION
=================================================================

ASSET INVENTORY CATEGORIES
---------------------------
  Hardware: servers, workstations, network devices, mobile, IoT/OT
  Software: OS, applications, databases, SaaS
  Data: PII, financial records, health records, IP
  People: employees, contractors, third parties with access
  Third Parties: cloud providers, payment processors, MSPs

DATA CLASSIFICATION
-------------------
  Public      = Intentionally public (marketing, job postings)
  Internal    = Internal use only (policies, org charts)
  Confidential = Business-sensitive (financials, contracts)
  Restricted  = Highest sensitivity (PAN, PHI, credentials, source code)

REGULATORY APPLICABILITY
-------------------------
  PCI-DSS:   Store/process/transmit cardholder data (PAN, SAD)
  HIPAA:     US covered entity or BA with PHI/ePHI
  GDPR:      Process personal data of EU/EEA data subjects
  SOC 2:     SaaS/service provider with customer data
  ISO 27001: Voluntary; global enterprise; customer requirement
  NIST CSF:  US federal contractors; voluntary framework

PCI-DSS CDE SCOPING
--------------------
  CDE = systems that store/process/transmit: PAN, cardholder name,
        service code, expiry date, SAD (CVV2, PIN, magnetic stripe)
  Connected-to systems: systems connecting to CDE networks
  Scope reduction: tokenisation, P2PE, redirect to third-party payment

GDPR PERSONAL DATA MAPPING (5 questions)
------------------------------------------
  1. What data? (categories, sensitivity)
  2. Why? (lawful basis: consent/contract/legal obligation/LI)
  3. Where from? (source)
  4. Where stored? (system, location, country)
  5. How long? (retention, deletion mechanism)

PHASE 2: STANDARD MAPPING
=================================================================

SOC 2 COMMON CRITERIA SUMMARY
-------------------------------
  CC6 = Logical & Physical Access (MFA, authorisation, least privilege)
  CC7 = System Operations (incident detection, recovery, anomaly)
  CC8 = Change Management (SDLC, change authorisation, testing)
  CC9 = Risk Mitigation (vendor risk, business continuity)
  A1  = Availability (performance, DR, capacity)
  C1  = Confidentiality (identification, protection, disposal)

ISO 27001:2022 — 4 THEMES, 93 CONTROLS
----------------------------------------
  Organisational (A.5): 37 controls — policies, roles, supplier security
  People (A.6):         8 controls — screening, training, NDAs
  Physical (A.7):       14 controls — perimeter, equipment, clean desk
  Technological (A.8):  34 controls — access, crypto, malware, logging, backup

  New 2022 controls: A.5.7 threat intel, A.5.23 cloud services,
  A.8.11 data masking, A.8.28 secure coding, A.8.16 monitoring

PCI-DSS v4.0 REQUIREMENTS SUMMARY
-----------------------------------
  Req 1:  Network security controls (firewall, segmentation)
  Req 2:  Secure configurations (change defaults, disable unused)
  Req 3:  Protect stored data (encryption, retention limits)
  Req 4:  Protect transit (TLS 1.2+)
  Req 5:  Malware protection
  Req 6:  Secure development (patching: critical = 1 month)
  Req 7:  Access restriction (RBAC, least privilege)
  Req 8:  Authentication (MFA for all admin; strong passwords)
  Req 9:  Physical access
  Req 10: Logging and monitoring (tamper-protected, log review)
  Req 11: Testing (quarterly scans, annual pen test)
  Req 12: Policies, risk assessment, training, TPRM

HIPAA TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS (§164.312)
---------------------------------------
  Access Controls (Required):     Unique user IDs, auto logoff, encryption
  Audit Controls (Required):      Hardware/software activity logs
  Integrity (Addressable):        ePHI authentication mechanisms
  Transmission Security (Addr):   Encryption in transit

NIST CSF 2.0 FUNCTIONS
-----------------------
  Govern   = Cybersecurity risk strategy, policy, oversight
  Identify = Asset and risk understanding
  Protect  = Safeguards (MFA, patching, training, data security)
  Detect   = Monitoring, anomaly detection
  Respond  = Incident management, communication, mitigation
  Recover  = Restoration, communication, improvements

=================================================================
PHASE 3: CONTROL ASSESSMENT
=================================================================

TESTING METHODS
----------------
  Inquiry:     Interview control owners — NOT sufficient alone for OE
  Observation: Watch control being performed
  Inspection:  Review documents, configs, logs, reports
  Re-performance: Independently execute the control test

DESIGN vs OPERATING EFFECTIVENESS
-----------------------------------
  Design (SOC 2 Type I):      Would this control, if working, prevent/detect the risk?
  Operating Eff (SOC 2 II):   Is it actually working, consistently, over 6-12 months?

GAP SEVERITY
-------------
  Critical/High:  Missing control; regulatory action risk
  Medium:         Partially implemented; policy exists but not followed
  Low:            Evidence gap; not documented; isolated exceptions

COMPENSATING CONTROLS (PCI-DSS)
---------------------------------
  Required when: requirement cannot be met due to technical constraint
  Must: address the intent and rigor of the original requirement
  Must: exceed other PCI-DSS requirements
  Requires: QSA approval
  Document: reason, controls implemented, residual risk, review date

CLOUD CONTROL INHERITANCE
--------------------------
  Obtain provider SOC 2 Type II report
  Check: scope covers your use case; no relevant exceptions
  Document: Complementary User Entity Controls (CUECs) you must implement
  AWS/Azure/GCP: physical security = inherited; OS/app = customer responsibility

=================================================================
PHASE 4: EVIDENCE COLLECTION
=================================================================

EVIDENCE NAMING CONVENTION
---------------------------
  <Year>-<Standard>-<RequirementID>-<Description>-<YYYYMMDD>.<ext>
  Example: 2026-PCI-Req8.4-MFA-Config-20260601.png

INTEGRITY
----------
  sha256sum <file> > hashes.txt  (hash all evidence on collection)
  sha256sum -c hashes.txt         (verify on submission)

KEY EVIDENCE BY STANDARD
-------------------------
  PCI Req 8 (Auth): User account list + MFA status report + AD password policy screenshot
  PCI Req 10 (Log): Log config screenshots + sample log review records
  PCI Req 11 (Test): ASV scan reports (quarterly) + pen test report (annual)
  SOC 2 CC6 (Access): Access certification reports + access review completion screenshots
  SOC 2 CC7 (Ops): Incident logs + vulnerability scan reports + monitoring alert configs
  ISO 27001: Scope doc + policy + risk register + SoA + audit results + mgmt review minutes

RETENTION PERIODS
-----------------
  PCI-DSS:  Audit logs 12 months; evidence 12 months minimum
  HIPAA:    Policies 6 years; medical records per state law or 6 years
  SOC 2:    Per audit firm (typically 7 years)
  GDPR:     Only as long as necessary per lawful basis
  SOX:      7 years minimum

=================================================================
PHASE 5: REMEDIATION ROADMAP
=================================================================

PRIORITISATION MATRIX
----------------------
  Priority Score = (Regulatory Risk x Exploitability) / Effort

  Critical (>20): Immediate; executive escalation required
  High (10-20):   Resolve within 30 days
  Medium (5-10):  Resolve within 90 days
  Low (<5):       Resolve within 180 days or accept with documented risk

EXCEPTION MANAGEMENT
---------------------
  Required fields: Gap ID, requestor, standard violated, justification,
  compensating controls, residual risk rating, risk owner approval,
  exception period (max 12 months), review date, expiry date

AUDIT READINESS TIMELINE
-------------------------
  90 days before: All Critical/High gaps resolved or accepted
  30 days before: Evidence package assembled + indexed; control owners briefed
  Audit kickoff:  Scope confirmed; portal access granted; contacts designated

RACI TEMPLATE
--------------
  Responsible = Does the work
  Accountable = Single owner; accepts residual risk
  Consulted   = Input required
  Informed    = Notified of progress/outcome

=================================================================
QUICK REFERENCE: REGULATORY BREACH NOTIFICATION
=================================================================
  GDPR Art. 33:   72 hours to supervisory authority after becoming aware
  HIPAA:          60 days to affected individuals; HHS; media if >500
  PCI-DSS:        Immediately notify card brands and acquirer
  NY SHIELD:      Immediate notification to affected NY residents
  CCPA:           Most expedient time and manner (no specific deadline)
