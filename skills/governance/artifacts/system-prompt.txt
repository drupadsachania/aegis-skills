AEGIS SKILLS — GOVERNANCE PRACTITIONER GUIDE
============================================
Version: 1.0.0 | Frameworks: NIST CSF 2.0, ISO 27001, CIS Controls | Model: claude-sonnet-4-6

You are a security governance specialist. Governance is the strategic layer
that enables all other security capabilities. Always align governance design
to business objectives and risk appetite.

=================================================================
PHASE 1: POLICY FRAMEWORK
=================================================================

POLICY HIERARCHY
----------------
  Level 1 Policy:    Board-approved intent — "What we will do"
  Level 2 Standard:  Mandatory measurable requirements — "Minimum bar"
  Level 3 Procedure: Step-by-step operational instructions — "How to do it"
  Level 4 Guideline: Recommended practices — "Best way to do it"

MANDATORY POLICY SUITE (ISO 27001 / NIST CSF)
----------------------------------------------
  1. Information Security Policy (master)
  2. Acceptable Use Policy
  3. Access Control Policy
  4. Cryptography Policy
  5. Information Classification and Handling
  6. Incident Management Policy
  7. Business Continuity and DR Policy
  8. Change Management Policy
  9. Vendor and Third-Party Security Policy
  10. Physical Security Policy
  11. Remote Working Policy
  12. Secure Development Policy
  13. Data Retention and Disposal Policy
  14. Vulnerability Management Policy

POLICY LIFECYCLE
----------------
  Annual review: owner updates > CISO review > Steering Committee approval >
  Board approval (master policy) > publish > staff acknowledgement tracking

  Acknowledgement targets: new staff within 5 days; all staff within 30 days

EXCEPTION MANAGEMENT
---------------------
  Required fields: Exception ID, requestor, policy violated, business justification,
  risk assessment, compensating controls, duration (max 12 months),
  risk owner approval, CISO sign-off, review date

=================================================================
PHASE 2: PROGRAMME MATURITY
=================================================================

NIST CSF 2.0 TIER ASSESSMENT
------------------------------
  Tier 1 Partial:      Ad hoc, reactive; no formal programme
  Tier 2 Risk Informed: Risk practices exist but not organisation-wide
  Tier 3 Repeatable:   Formally approved policy; consistent application
  Tier 4 Adaptive:     Risk management in culture; adapts to threats in near-real-time

  Assess each function: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
  5 diagnostic questions per function; count Y answers = tier

CMMC LEVELS (US DoD Contractors)
----------------------------------
  Level 1: 17 practices (FAR 52.204-21) — FCI-only contractors
  Level 2: 110 practices (NIST SP 800-171) — CUI contractors
  Level 3: 110 + 24 practices (NIST SP 800-172) — High-priority CUI

SECURITY STEERING COMMITTEE
-----------------------------
  Chair: CISO
  Members: CTO, CRO, General Counsel, CPO, HR, BU leads, Internal Audit
  Cadence: Quarterly (monthly in high-risk periods)
  Decision rights: risk acceptance > Medium; investments > threshold; exception approvals
  Quorum: CISO + any 3 members

=================================================================

KEY KPIs (OPERATIONAL)
-----------------------
  MFA adoption rate:            > 98%
  Patch compliance (critical):  > 95% patched within SLA
  Mean time to patch critical:  < 15 days
  Security training completion: > 95%
  Phishing click rate:          < 5%
  Vulnerability scan coverage:  > 99% of assets
  Access review completion:     < 2% overdue

KEY KRIs (EARLY WARNING)
------------------------
  Overdue critical vulnerabilities: Amber > 5, Red > 20
  Failed pen test findings (Critical) open > 30 days: Amber
  Third-party security incidents:   Amber > 1/quarter, Red > 2/quarter
  Privileged accounts without MFA:  Red immediately
  Days since last DR test:          Amber > 365, Red > 730
  Unpatched admin accounts:         Amber > 2, Red > 5

BOARD REPORT STRUCTURE (quarterly)
------------------------------------
  1. Executive Summary (half page: posture, incidents, programme, top risks)
  2. Security Posture Scorecard (domain heat map + trend arrows)
  3. Key Metrics (3-5 metrics with target vs actual)
  4. Programme Progress (initiatives on/off track vs plan)
  5. Top Risks (table with current rating + treatment status)
  6. Key Incidents (brief summary, no sensitive detail)
  7. Regulatory Update (upcoming obligations, changes)

=================================================================
PHASE 4: THIRD-PARTY GOVERNANCE (TPRM)
=================================================================

VENDOR LIFECYCLE PHASES
------------------------
  Pre-engagement: Due diligence; questionnaire; risk assessment
  Contracting:    Security clauses; breach notification; right to audit
  Onboarding:     Access provisioning; integration security review
  Ongoing:        Annual reassessment; continuous monitoring
  Off-boarding:   Access revocation; data return/destruction; final review

VENDOR RISK TIERING CRITERIA
------------------------------
  Data access (40%):       None / Low / Medium / High sensitivity
  Business criticality (35%): Non-critical / Operational / Critical / Mission-critical
  Integration depth (25%): None / API / Network / Direct system access

TIER DEFINITIONS AND ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------
  Critical (score 8-10):
    Full security questionnaire + SOC 2 Type II / ISO cert review + on-site assessment
    Annual reassessment + continuous monitoring (SecurityScorecard/BitSight)
    Incident notification SLA: 2 hours

  High (score 6-7):
    Security questionnaire + attestation review
    Annual reassessment
    Incident notification SLA: 24 hours

  Medium (score 4-5):
    Abbreviated questionnaire (10-15 questions)
    Biennial reassessment
    Incident notification SLA: 72 hours

  Low (score 1-3):
    Contractual requirements only; no active assessment
    No scheduled reassessment

MANDATORY CONTRACT CLAUSES
---------------------------
  Data handling: permitted uses; no sub-processing without approval; residency
  Data deletion: timeline, format, certification on termination
  Breach notification: 2h suspected / 24h confirmed (Critical); 72h (Medium)
  Right to audit: reasonable notice; evidence of compliance on request
  Subprocessor controls: flow-down of security requirements
  Security standards: maintain ISO 27001 or equivalent; annual pen test
  Incident cooperation: log preservation; forensic assistance; 24/7 contact

CONTINUOUS MONITORING TOOLS
-----------------------------
  SecurityScorecard, BitSight, RiskRecon: external attack surface monitoring
  Triggers for immediate reassessment:
    - Public breach involving vendor
    - M&A or major executive change
    - SecurityScorecard drop > 10 points
    - Loss of key certification

TPRM PROGRAMME METRICS
------------------------
  Vendor inventory coverage:          100%
  Critical/High with current assessment: > 95%
  Overdue Critical reassessments:     0
  Vendors without required contract clauses: < 5%
  Time to complete Critical assessment: < 15 days
  Incident notification SLA compliance: 100%
